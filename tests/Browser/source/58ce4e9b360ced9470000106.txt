<h2 id="about-this-kata-series">About this Kata Series</h2>
<p>This Kata Series explores a core API in PHP called <a href="http://php.net/manual/en/book.reflection.php" target="_blank">Reflection</a> which allows a developer to <em>reverse-engineer</em> classes, interfaces, functions, methods and extensions.  It is assumed that the user undertaking this Kata Series is already familiar with both functional and object-oriented programming in PHP.  A good indicator that you are ready for this Kata Series is if you are able to complete at least <strong>6 out of 8</strong> topics/Kata in my <a href="https://www.codewars.com/collections/php-functions" target="_blank">PHP Functions</a> series without hesitation and are at least moderately familiar with all the topics covered in the <strong>first 7 Kata</strong> in my <a href="https://www.codewars.com/collections/object-oriented-php" target="_blank">Object-Oriented PHP</a> series.  Certain Kata in this Series may also require slightly more advanced OOP knowledge such as the awareness of what an interface is, but if such extra knowledge is required, it will be mentioned under a "Prerequisites" subtitle.</p>
<p>Most Kata in this Series will consist of two main parts - a "Lesson" and a "Task".  However, unlike either of "Object-Oriented PHP" and "PHP Functions", the "Lesson" will only teach the fundamental concepts required to <strong>understand</strong> the "Task" presented in each Kata.  In most cases, you will be expected to look up on <a href="http://php.net/manual/en/book.reflection.php" target="_blank">the official Reflection documentation</a> in order to figure out the details of how to actually complete the Kata "Task" (e.g. which methods to call or even what class to use!).  If you are still in doubt after referring to the official documentation, you are <strong>strongly encouraged</strong> to conduct your own research and use whatever resources are available to you on the Internet or otherwise.</p>
<h2 id="prerequisites">Prerequisites</h2>
<p>In addition to the prerequisites mentioned under the heading "About this Kata Series", you will also be expected to be aware of <strong>anonymous classes</strong> and how to instantiate objects from them.  If you have absolutely no clue what I am talking about, consider <a href="https://www.codewars.com/kata/57cd5b1258da9e828000059d" target="_blank">completing this Kata</a> before attempting this challenge.</p>
<h2 id="kata-overview">Kata Overview</h2>
<p>This Kata is the final Kata of this Series and as such is intended as a light-hearted assessment in order to test yourself on how well you have learned and understood the concepts taught in previous Kata of this Series.  You will not be expected to use any Reflection API outside of those covered in all previous Kata of this Series (i.e. <code>ReflectionFunction</code>, <code>ReflectionParameter</code>, <code>ReflectionClass</code>, <code>ReflectionProperty</code> and <code>ReflectionMethod</code>); however, you may be expected to call on methods that you haven't done so before and sometimes in an ingenious way that may not be initially apparent.  Don't worry, this "Assessment" will be fun as it is presented in the form of a ... <strong>Puzzle Challenge</strong>!!!</p>
<h2 id="assessment">Assessment</h2>
<p>The assessment is split into three sections, or rather, <strong>code challenges</strong>.  You must pass all three of them in order to complete this Kata.</p>
<h3 id="code-challenge-1---find-the-odd-one-out">Code Challenge #1 - Find the odd one out</h3>
<p>Preloaded for you is a non-associative array <code>$functions</code> which contains the names of 30 named functions (each as a string).  If you perform Reflection on each and every function named in <code>$functions</code>, you should discover that all of them have the same function signature (except for their names of course) <strong>with the exception of one of them</strong>.  Find the odd one out and once you think you've figured out which is the unique one, assign the name of that function to the global <code>$answer</code> variable.</p>
<h3 id="code-challenge-2---the-curious-case-of-the-anonymous-twins">Code Challenge #2 - The Curious Case of the Anonymous Twins</h3>
<p>We all know that anonymous classes are the way to go when you want to instantiate one-off objects without polluting the global namespace, but is it possible to instantiate more than one object from a single anonymous class?  Your goal is to create two <strong>separate, distinct</strong> objects, <code>$twin1</code> and <code>$twin2</code>, which are <strong>both instances of the same anonymous class</strong>.</p>
<h3 id="code-challenge-3---diffuse-that-bomb">Code Challenge #3 - Diffuse That Bomb</h3>
<p>Your archnemesis hates you so much that he/she has planted a bomb (class <code>Bomb</code>) in your house/apartment!  When you discover the bomb in your apartment, you realise that it is already too late to simply chuck it out of the house/apartment as it is set to explode within 60 seconds.  Fortunately, one of your secret skills is the ability to diffuse any bomb quickly so there is still hope.</p>
<p>Preloaded for you is a class <code>Bomb</code> with 30 distinct wires (class methods).  Your job is to search for the correct wire and cut that wire <strong>only</strong> (call on the correct class method) which will diffuse the bomb and save your life (and the precious computer next to you).  If you accidentally cut the wrong wire (call on an incorrect class method), you will cause the bomb to explode immediately so take care!  The wire you're looking for has the following appearance (method signature):</p>
<ul>
<li>Is declared with the keywords <code>final</code>, <code>public</code> and <code>static</code></li>
<li>Has exactly <strong>one</strong> parameter which is <strong>optional</strong></li>
<li>The parameter has a declared type of <code>float</code> and defaults to <code>M_PI</code></li>
<li>Has a return type of <code>int</code></li>
</ul>
<p>Only <strong>one</strong> of the wires will satisfy all of the conditions above.  Hurry!  Now that you've read this Description you've only got 15 seconds left!  Tick ... tock ... tick ... tock ... </p>
